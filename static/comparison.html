05 · Platform by platform

A fair look at
every competitor.

We don't win by misrepresenting what others do. These are honest assessments of what each platform does well and where they fall short.

HereAfter AI
Active

A US-based voice chatbot that lets you record yourself answering questions, then lets family members ask those questions after you're gone. Pre-recorded audio responses, not generative AI. No video, no India support.

Where it's strong
  • Good for pre-planned legacy audio interviews
  • Established product, emotionally thoughtful prompts
  • Simple to use for non-technical families
Where it falls short
  • No India pricing, UPI, or Razorpay support
  • Audio only — no video, no live face animation
  • Pre-recorded responses, not open-ended AI
  • Cannot create a replica from existing video
  • Zero self-knowledge or pattern intelligence features
Best for: Living people recording messages for family in advance. Chronis is the next step when they want something generative, visual, and alive.
StoryFile
Bankrupt

A pioneering company behind interactive Holocaust survivor memorials. Filed for bankruptcy in early 2026. Product access is uncertain. Their approach — pre-recorded video interviews — was the right direction, but the technology didn't generate. Chronis does.

Where it was strong
  • Video format — face visible, not just audio
  • Archival mission, culturally significant use cases
  • Interview-based depth of content
Current status
  • Filed for bankruptcy — product future uncertain
  • No generative AI — all responses were pre-recorded
  • Never had India availability or ₹ pricing
  • No pattern intelligence, no self-knowledge
Best for: Nothing currently. Existing StoryFile users: Chronis is a natural migration path for live, generative conversation.
Project December
Active

A text-based GPT-powered platform. Became notable when people used it to simulate deceased loved ones. No voice, no face, no India support. The interface is a chat window. The persona is a prompt, not a person.

Where it's strong
  • Low barrier to entry — no upload or hardware needed
  • Generative AI — open-ended conversation
  • Available globally
Where it falls short
  • Text only — no voice, no face, no physical presence
  • Generic AI persona — not grounded in real signal
  • No India pricing, no self-knowledge, no pattern AI
  • Minimal ethics framework or psychological safeguards
Best for: First-contact with grief tech. Chronis is where you go when text is no longer enough and you want something real.
Replika
Not grief-specific

A well-funded AI companion app. Strong India presence. Not designed for deceased person simulation or self-quantification — some users have attempted both with mixed and sometimes harmful results, due to the complete absence of grief-specific design.

Where it's strong
  • Polished, mature product with good India reach
  • ₹ pricing and regional availability
  • Voice interaction available
Why it doesn't work for this
  • Generic AI persona — not your person
  • No voice clone, no face, no real-signal grounding
  • No self-quantification or pattern intelligence
  • No grief-specific psychological safeguards
Best for: AI companionship generally. People using Replika for grief are working around a tool that wasn't designed for it — which carries real risk.
