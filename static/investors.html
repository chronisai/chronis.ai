Investment thesis

Why this category
becomes large.

Grief is permanent. The desire to preserve presence is permanent. Technology has finally caught up to the depth of that desire.

01 · The category

Grief tech is an inevitable category — not a niche

Every human being will lose someone they love. The shift from passive memory (photos, videos) to interactive presence is the category-defining moment. Chronis is the first product to make that shift real.

02 · The timing

AI capability crossed the emotional fidelity threshold in 2025

Real-time voice cloning, lip-sync video, and personality-grounded language models all crossed the quality threshold for emotional credibility within the last 18 months. The timing is not incidental — it is precise.

03 · The India wedge

India is the largest underserved market in this category

1.4 billion people. Deep ancestral reverence culture. No domestic grief tech platform. Every competitor is built for US users with USD pricing. Chronis is India-native by design. The gap is structural.

04 · The moat

Memory data creates compounding user lock-in

Once a family uploads a deceased loved one's video, that replica becomes irreplaceable. No switching cost is higher than losing continued access to a deceased parent's voice and face.

05 · The demand signal

12+ countries in week one with zero ad spend

When a product designed for India gets organic sessions from 12+ countries in week one, it signals that grief transcends geography. The India wedge is a market entry — the TAM is global.

06 · The format advantage

Video is categorically different from text or audio

Project December is text only. HereAfter AI is audio only. StoryFile was pre-recorded video. Chronis is the only platform with real-time, generative, lip-synced video conversation. The emotional delta is not incremental — it is transformative.

The highest switching cost in consumer software is not price. It is the fear of losing something irreplaceable.
